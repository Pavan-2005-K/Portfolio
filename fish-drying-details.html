SocioSpark 2K25 Social Innovation Hackathon Entry

Motivation and Problem

Our team (Creative Crew) built this device during the SocioSpark 2K25, a 24-hour Social Innovation Hackathon. The project was motivated by the slow, unhygienic, and contamination-prone practice of drying fish on coir mats, which limits market value.

Our Solution

We developed a hygienic solar-assisted fish dryer that revolutionizes traditional methods by drying fish faster and in a hygienic manner.

Technology & Components

  • Hygienic Drying: Uses stainless steel trays.
  • Monitoring & Control: Utilizes ESP32-controlled fans and DHT22 sensors for real-time monitoring and optimized drying.
  • Reliability: Powered by solar energy with a battery backup for off-grid operation.

This innovation ensures food safety, helps meet export standards, and ultimately increases the value of the fish product.
